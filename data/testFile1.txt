========================================
ACME CORPORATION INTERNAL EMPLOYEE GUIDE
Version 3.2 — Updated January 2026
========================================

SECTION 1 — WORKING HOURS

Employees are expected to work 40 hours per week. Standard working hours are from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday.

Flexible scheduling is available for employees who have completed their probation period. Employees may begin work between 7:00 AM and 11:00 AM as long as they complete their required daily hours.

Remote employees must remain available on Slack during core collaboration hours between 11:00 AM and 3:00 PM CET.

Overtime must be approved by a direct manager before being performed. Unauthorized overtime may not be compensated.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — VACATION POLICY

Full-time employees receive 26 paid vacation days per calendar year.

Employees who have worked at the company for less than 12 months receive vacation days proportionally based on their start date.

Unused vacation days may be carried over into the next year, but they must be used before March 31st.

Vacation requests longer than 10 consecutive business days require approval from both the direct manager and HR.

During the final two weeks of December, no more than 30% of a department may be on vacation simultaneously.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — SICK LEAVE

Employees must notify their manager before 10:00 AM on the first day of absence.

A medical certificate is required for sick leave longer than 3 consecutive days.

Employees on sick leave are not expected to respond to emails or attend meetings.

The company provides up to 30 paid sick leave days annually.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — TRAVEL REIMBURSEMENT

Business travel must be booked through the corporate travel portal.

Hotel reimbursement is limited to:
- 600 PLN per night inside Poland
- 180 EUR per night within the European Union
- 250 USD per night outside the European Union

Meal reimbursements:
- Breakfast: 40 PLN
- Lunch: 70 PLN
- Dinner: 90 PLN

Taxi reimbursement requires a digital receipt.

First-class flights are not reimbursed unless approved by a department director.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — SECURITY POLICY

Employees must use multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all company systems.

Passwords must:
- contain at least 14 characters
- include uppercase and lowercase letters
- include at least one number
- include at least one special character

Sharing credentials with another employee is strictly prohibited.

External USB devices may not be connected to company laptops without approval from IT Security.

Employees must lock their workstation when leaving their desk.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT GUIDELINES

All backend services must expose REST APIs documented using OpenAPI specifications.

Python services should follow async-first design principles where appropriate.

Production services must include:
- structured logging
- centralized error handling
- health check endpoints
- request tracing
- retry policies for external API calls

Pull requests require at least one approving reviewer before merging.

Direct commits to the main branch are prohibited.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — AI SYSTEMS POLICY

Employees developing AI systems must document:
- model provider
- prompt templates
- tool definitions
- evaluation metrics
- fallback mechanisms

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems should:
- use chunk overlap between 10% and 20%
- store embeddings in approved vector databases
- validate retrieved context before generation
- log retrieval failures

AI agents with tool access must not:
- execute shell commands
- access production credentials
- send external emails without approval

All AI-generated customer responses must include confidence scoring metadata.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 8 — INCIDENT RESPONSE

Security incidents must be reported within 15 minutes of discovery.

Critical incidents are classified as:
- data leaks
- credential compromise
- ransomware detection
- unauthorized production access

Incident severity levels:
- SEV-1: Full outage or major breach
- SEV-2: Partial degradation
- SEV-3: Minor issue with workaround

SEV-1 incidents require executive notification within 30 minutes.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 9 — PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS

Performance reviews occur twice per year:
- June
- December

Evaluation categories include:
- technical execution
- collaboration
- communication
- ownership
- innovation

Employees scoring “Exceptional” for two consecutive review cycles become eligible for accelerated promotion review.

Managers must submit written evaluations no later than 7 business days before review meetings.

----------------------------------------

SECTION 10 — EQUIPMENT POLICY

Employees receive:
- one company laptop
- one external monitor
- one keyboard
- one mouse

Replacement cycle:
- laptops every 3 years
- monitors every 5 years

Lost equipment must be reported immediately.

Intentional damage to company equipment may result in disciplinary action.
